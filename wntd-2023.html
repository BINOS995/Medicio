About World No Tobacco Day 2023

2023 Theme: We Need Food, Not Tobacco

The World Health Organization's theme for World No Tobacco Day 2023 focuses on the environmental impact of tobacco growing, production, and waste, highlighting how valuable land and resources are diverted from food production to tobacco cultivation.

World No Tobacco Day (WNTD) is observed around the world every year on May 31. It is intended to encourage a 24-hour period of abstinence from all forms of tobacco consumption around the globe. The day is further intended to draw attention to the widespread prevalence of tobacco use and to negative health effects, which currently lead to more than 8 million deaths each year worldwide, including 1.2 million deaths from exposure to second-hand smoke.

The 2023 campaign aims to:

  • Raise awareness about alternative farming approaches and crops that are more sustainable than tobacco for farmers
  • Expose the tobacco industry's efforts to interfere with attempts to substitute tobacco growing with sustainable crops
  • Highlight the industry's efforts to "greenwash" its reputation by promoting activities under the guise of corporate social responsibility

Vision for Accelerated Sustainable Development Ghana has organized several activities and events to mark this important day and raise awareness about the harmful effects of tobacco use and the environmental impact of tobacco cultivation.

Key Facts

  • Tobacco kills up to half of its users
  • Tobacco kills more than 8 million people each year
  • Over 1.2 million deaths are the result of non-smokers being exposed to second-hand smoke
  • Around 80% of the world's 1.3 billion tobacco users live in low- and middle-income countries
  • Tobacco farming uses 200,000 hectares of land in Africa that could be used to grow food
