Documented Industry Interference

Explore documented cases of tobacco and alcohol industry interference in public health policies across Ghana and Africa. These cases highlight tactics used by industries to undermine health policies and our responses to protect public health.

British American Tobacco Ghana Lobbying

2023-2024 Ghana Parliament Documented

BAT Ghana conducted extensive lobbying against tobacco tax increases through direct meetings with MPs, funded "research" claiming economic harm, and orchestrated industry-friendly media coverage.

Impact on Public Health:

Delayed crucial tobacco tax legislation by 8 months, potentially leading to increased smoking rates among youth and reduced government revenue for healthcare.

Our Response:

Exposed lobbying tactics through media briefings, provided MPs with independent economic analysis, mobilized civil society support for tax increases.

Alcohol Industry Opposition to Marketing Restrictions

2024 Ministry of Health Ongoing

Alcohol industry associations launched coordinated opposition to proposed restrictions on alcohol marketing, including social media campaigns targeting youth and claims of economic devastation.

Impact on Public Health:

Successfully watered down marketing restrictions, allowing continued exposure of children to alcohol advertising through digital platforms.

Our Response:

Documented industry tactics, provided evidence of youth exposure to alcohol marketing, advocated for stronger restrictions through public health coalitions.

Philip Morris Legal Challenge to Plain Packaging

2022-2023 Regional Court Resolved

Philip Morris International filed legal challenges against Ghana's plain packaging regulations, claiming violations of intellectual property rights and international trade agreements.

Impact on Public Health:

Delayed implementation of plain packaging by 12 months, allowing continued attractive cigarette packaging that appeals to youth.

Our Response:

Provided legal support to government, submitted evidence on plain packaging effectiveness, mobilized international public health support for Ghana's position.

Japan Tobacco International Media Manipulation

2024 National Media Documented

JTI orchestrated media campaigns presenting misleading economic data on tobacco taxation, funded "independent" economic reports, and planted op-eds in major newspapers.

Impact on Public Health:

Created public confusion about tobacco tax benefits, influenced editorial positions of major media outlets against tax increases.

Our Response:

Fact-checked industry claims, provided accurate economic data to journalists, exposed industry funding sources through investigative reporting.

Diageo Legal Challenge to Alcohol Warning Labels

2023-2024 Supreme Court Ongoing

Diageo and other alcohol companies filed constitutional challenges against mandatory cancer warning labels on alcohol products, claiming violations of commercial speech rights.

Impact on Public Health:

Blocked implementation of cancer warnings, leaving consumers uninformed about alcohol-related cancer risks.

Our Response:

Provided scientific evidence on alcohol-cancer links, supported government legal defense, mobilized cancer survivors and health professionals.

Alliance for Consumer Choice Front Group

2023 Parliament Hearings Documented

Tobacco industry created and funded "Alliance for Consumer Choice" to oppose tobacco control measures while presenting as grassroots consumer advocacy group.

Impact on Public Health:

Provided false grassroots opposition to tobacco control, influenced parliamentary committee hearings against stronger regulations.

Our Response:

Exposed industry funding and connections, revealed front group nature through investigative research, provided MPs with accurate information.
